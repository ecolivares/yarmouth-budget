# Yarmouth Budget — Phase 1 Findings

*FY2024 (approved) → FY2027 (draft). All figures from the validated master dataset.*

## 1. Where the money goes — FY27 General Fund = **$65.58M**
| | $ | Share |
|---|--:|--:|
| **Education (schools)** | $44.11M | **67.3%** |
| **Municipal (town)** | $19.56M | 29.8% |
| **Cumberland County tax** | $1.90M | 2.9% |

**Schools are 2/3 of the budget** — any large tax change runs through them.
Inside the $44.1M school budget: Regular Program **$19.8M (45%)**, Special Ed
**$7.4M (17%)**, Operations & Maintenance $3.6M, Debt $2.5M, Principals' offices
$1.9M, Transportation $1.8M, Technology $1.4M, Athletics $1.1M.

Inside the $19.56M municipal budget: General Government $4.95M (Benefits alone
$3.06M), Public Safety $4.53M (Police $1.66M, Fire $1.38M), Public Works $4.10M,
Capital Programs $3.39M (debt $1.57M + reserves $1.82M), Public Services $2.48M
(Library, Parks), Health & Welfare just $0.11M.

## 2. Growth — the core story
| | FY24 → FY27 | Δ | CAGR |
|---|---|--:|--:|
| Total General Fund | $54.86M → $65.58M | +$10.7M (+19.5%) | +6.1%/yr |
| Education | $36.59M → $44.11M | +$7.5M (+20.6%) | +6.4%/yr |
| Municipal | $16.74M → $19.56M | +$2.8M (+16.8%) | +5.3%/yr |
| County tax | $1.53M → $1.90M | +$0.4M (+24.9%) | +7.7%/yr |
| **Net property-tax levy** | **$39.30M → $48.76M** | **+$9.5M (+24.1%)** | **+7.5%/yr** |

The **levy is growing ~7.5%/yr — faster than the budget (6.1%)** because non-tax
revenue is growing slower than spending (it covered 26.8% of the budget in FY24
but only 24.5% in FY27), so a rising share falls on property taxes.

Fastest-growing municipal categories: Public Services +23.6%, General Government
+22.4%, Public Safety +19.7%. Capital was nearly flat (+2.2%); Health & Welfare
fell (general-assistance normalization).

## 2b. The school budget is overwhelmingly people (FY27 object-line detail)
From the 1,218-line level-4 school budget, by spend type:
| Spend type | $ | Share |
|---|--:|--:|
| Salaries & wages | $28.48M | 64.6% |
| Employee benefits | $8.08M | 18.3% |
| Other / dues / debt | $2.54M | 5.8% |
| Property services | $1.71M | 3.9% |
| Supplies | $1.55M | 3.5% |
| All other purchased svcs / equip | $1.74M | 4.0% |
| **Personnel (salaries + benefits)** | **$36.57M** | **82.9%** |

**~83% of the school budget — and over half the entire town budget — is staff
compensation.** This is the central constraint on cutting: meaningful school
savings means fewer positions or smaller raises/benefits, not trimming supplies.

## 3. What's actually driving the increases (municipal line items, FY24→FY27)
Top movers are **personnel and benefits**, not discretionary programs:
Health & Dental insurance **+$289k**, Police wages +$201k, Fire wages +$194k,
Retirement +$159k, Admin wages +$153k, Hydrant rental +$143k, Bond principal
+$137k, Library wages +$121k, FICA +$108k. New positions show up too
(Sustainability Coordinator +$95k). **Salaries + benefits dominate** — the
budget is mostly contractual/headcount, which is why trimming is hard.

## 4. ⚠️ The revaluation trap
The tax **rate** fell $25.67 → $14.55 between FY25 and FY26. That is **not a tax
cut** — the town revalued property and the tax base ~doubled ($1.68B → $3.21B).
The **levy rose** every year. Always compare the **levy ($ raised)**, never the
rate, across the reval.

## 5. Property-tax mechanics (FY27)
- Tax base **$3.221B**, rate **$15.25/$1,000**, standard home **$500,000 → $7,625/yr**.
- The FY27 draft as proposed **adds ≈ +$375/yr** to that home's bill.
- **Conversion factor:** every **$1,000,000** cut lowers the rate **$0.31/$1,000**
  and the $500k home's bill by **≈ $155/year**.

## 6. Canned cut scenarios (savings on the $500k example home)
| Scenario | Cut | Rate Δ | $/home/yr |
|---|--:|--:|--:|
| Flat-fund **schools** at FY26 (0% vs +4.4%) | $1.86M | −$0.58 | **−$289** |
| Flat-fund **school + town** at FY26 (county is fixed) | $2.63M | −$0.82 | **−$408** |
| Hold **total** increase to 2.5% (inflation) | $1.28M | −$0.40 | −$199 |
| Eliminate **school athletics** entirely | $1.11M | −$0.34 | −$172 |
| Flat-fund **municipal** at FY26 (0% vs +4.1%) | $0.76M | −$0.24 | −$119 |
| Cut municipal **capital reserves** 50% | $0.91M | −$0.28 | −$141 |
| 5% across-the-board to **municipal operating** | $0.81M | −$0.25 | −$125 |
| Eliminate the **Community Services** dept | $0.39M | −$0.12 | −$60 |
| Double **use of surplus** (+$750k, **one-time**) | $0.75M | −$0.23 | −$116 |

### Takeaways for the cut conversation
- **The math is unforgiving.** Even erasing the *entire* FY27 school + town
  increase only saves ~$408/yr on a $500k home — and just freezes the bill near
  FY26, it doesn't lower it.
- **"Easy" targets are small.** Killing athletics or a whole department moves the
  bill < $175/yr each. ~85% of the budget is schools, public safety, benefits,
  debt, and capital.
- **Schools are the only big lever** (67% of spend); every 1% of the school
  budget ≈ $441k ≈ −$0.14 rate ≈ −$68/home. But **83% of that budget is
  personnel**, so a real school cut means staffing/compensation — supplies and
  programs (athletics 2.5%, technology 3.3%) are too small to move the rate much.
- **Revenue side matters too:** non-tax revenue is losing ground; growing it (or
  drawing more surplus) cuts the levy dollar-for-dollar, but surplus is one-time.

*These nine are starting points for the Phase-2 interactive explorer; the
conversion factor lets any custom cut be turned into a per-home number instantly.*
